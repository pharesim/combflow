1. Overview

HiveComb indexes and displays content from the public Hive blockchain. We do not host user-generated content — posts and comments are stored on the decentralised Hive blockchain and merely displayed through our interface.

Nevertheless, we take reports of illegal or rights-infringing content seriously. If content displayed on HiveComb infringes your rights, you may request that we remove it from our index using the process described below.

2. What We Can and Cannot Do

What we CAN do:

  • Remove specific posts from the HiveComb index so they no longer appear on our site.
  • Remove classification metadata (categories, sentiment, language labels) associated with a post.
  • Exclude a specific author from our index.

What we CANNOT do:

  • Delete or modify content on the Hive blockchain — it is a decentralised, immutable ledger.
  • Prevent the content from appearing on other Hive interfaces (e.g. PeakD, Ecency, Hive.blog).
  • Identify anonymous blockchain authors beyond their public Hive username.

3. Grounds for Removal

You may submit a takedown notice if content displayed on HiveComb:

  • Infringes your copyright or other intellectual property rights.
  • Violates your privacy rights (e.g. contains personal data you did not consent to publish).
  • Is defamatory and constitutes actionable defamation under EEA law.
  • Is illegal under the laws of the European Economic Area or applicable international law (e.g. illegal hate speech, child sexual abuse material, terrorist content).
  • Violates a court order or regulatory decision directed at us.

4. How to Submit a Notice

Send your takedown notice to: hivecomb@pharesim.me

Your notice must include:

  1. Your identity: Full name, email address, and (if representing an organisation) your role and the organisation's name.
  2. The content: The specific URL(s) on HiveComb displaying the content you want removed, or the author/permlink identifiers.
  3. The grounds: A clear explanation of why the content should be removed, referencing the applicable ground from section 3.
  4. Evidence (if applicable):
    • For copyright claims: proof of ownership (registration, original work, etc.).
    • For privacy claims: explanation of what personal data is involved and why its publication is unlawful.
    • For court orders: a copy of the order.
  5. Declaration of good faith: A statement that you believe in good faith that the content is infringing or illegal, and that the information in your notice is accurate.

5. Our Process

StepTimeframeAction
1. Acknowledgement Within 7 business days We confirm receipt of your notice and assess whether it is complete. Incomplete notices may be returned for clarification.
2. Assessment Within 30 days We review the notice and the content. Complex cases (e.g. contested copyright, cross-jurisdictional issues) may require additional time. For manifestly illegal content (e.g. CSAM, terrorist content), we act without undue delay.
3. Action After assessment If the notice is valid, we remove the content from our index. If we determine the notice is unfounded, we inform you of our reasoning.
4. Notification With action We notify you of the outcome. Where possible and legally appropriate, we may also notify the content author (via their public Hive account) that their content has been de-indexed.

6. Counter-Notice

If your content has been removed from the HiveComb index and you believe this was in error, you may submit a counter-notice to hivecomb@pharesim.me containing:

  1. Your Hive username and contact email.
  2. The content that was removed (URL or author/permlink).
  3. A clear explanation of why you believe the removal was unjustified.
  4. A statement that you consent to resolve the dispute in good faith.

We will review counter-notices within 30 days and may reinstate the content if we determine the original notice was unfounded.

7. Repeat Infringers

We may permanently exclude from our index any Hive author who is the subject of multiple valid takedown notices.

8. Abuse of Process

Submitting knowingly false or misleading takedown notices may result in legal liability. We reserve the right to disregard notices that are clearly abusive, vexatious, or submitted in bad faith.

9. Compliance

We provide this notice-and-takedown mechanism as our content moderation process. We maintain records of takedown notices and actions taken, and will cooperate with competent authorities as required by law.

Our point of contact for content moderation matters is: hivecomb@pharesim.me
